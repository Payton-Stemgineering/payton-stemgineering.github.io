Resources

Explore Exciting STEM Opportunities!

Looking to dive deeper into science, technology, engineering, or math? Whether you're aiming to build new skills, connect with like-minded peers, or get hands-on experience, these resources are a great way to immerse yourself in the world of STEM. Check out the opportunities below to find the perfect fit for your interests and goals!

Online Learning Platforms

    MIT OpenCourseWare – Free lecture notes and courses from MIT.
    Code.org – Accessible coding lessons for all ages.
    Brilliant.org – Interactive problem-solving in math, science, and engineering.
    NASA STEM Engagement – Free projects, challenges, and activities from NASA.
    CS50 by Harvard (edX) – A free intro to computer science course (great for high school/college prep).

Hands-On Activities & Projects

    Science Buddies – Thousands of STEM project ideas with step-by-step instructions.
    TeachEngineering – Engineering design challenges curated by universities.
    Exploratorium Tinkering Studio – Creative hands-on science activities.
    TryEngineering (IEEE) – Student-friendly projects and career resources.
    Howtoons – Comic-style DIY science and engineering builds.

Games & Interactive Tools

    PhET Interactive Simulations (University of Colorado Boulder) – Virtual experiments in physics, chemistry, biology, math.
    Foldit – Online protein-folding game blending biology and puzzle-solving.
    Kerbal Space Program – Spaceflight and physics simulation game.
    Math Playground – Fun logic and problem-solving games for younger students.
